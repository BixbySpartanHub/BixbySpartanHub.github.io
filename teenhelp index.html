üí≠ Mental Health

üò∞ Anxiety & Panic

Learn breathing techniques, grounding exercises, and when to seek help for overwhelming worry.

Quick Help:
  • 4-7-8 Breathing: Breathe in for 4, hold for 7, out for 8
  • 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding: Name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste
  • Progressive Muscle Relaxation: Tense and release muscle groups from toes to head

When to get help: If anxiety interferes with school, relationships, or daily activities for more than 2 weeks.

üòî Depression & Low Mood

Understanding depression, building coping skills, and finding hope during difficult times.

Signs to Watch For:
  • Persistent sadness or emptiness
  • Loss of interest in activities you used to enjoy
  • Changes in sleep or appetite
  • Feeling worthless or hopeless
Small Steps That Help:
  • Set one tiny goal each day (make your bed, take a shower)
  • Get sunlight for 10-15 minutes daily
  • Connect with one person, even briefly

üë´ Relationships & Social

üò° Bullying & Harassment

Strategies for dealing with bullies, reporting options, and rebuilding confidence.

Types of Bullying:
  • Physical: Hitting, pushing, damaging property
  • Verbal: Name-calling, threats, insults
  • Social: Exclusion, spreading rumors, humiliation
  • Cyberbullying: Online harassment, sharing private info
What You Can Do:
  • Document incidents (dates, witnesses, screenshots)
  • Report to trusted adults or authorities
  • Block/unfriend on social media
  • Travel with friends when possible
üíï Dating & Relationships

Healthy relationships, setting boundaries, and navigating breakups with self-respect.

Signs of a Healthy Relationship:
  • Mutual respect and trust
  • Open, honest communication
  • Support for each other's goals and friendships
  • Feeling comfortable being yourself
Red Flags to Watch:
  • Controlling behavior or extreme jealousy
  • Pressuring you sexually or otherwise
  • Isolating you from friends/family
  • Any form of abuse (physical, emotional, sexual)

üìö School & Life

üìñ Academic Pressure

Managing stress about grades, college prep, and finding balance in your academic life.

Study Smart, Not Hard:
  • Use the Pomodoro Technique (25 min work, 5 min break)
  • Create a dedicated study space
  • Break big projects into smaller tasks
  • Form study groups with classmates
Managing Test Anxiety:
  • Arrive early and bring everything you need
  • Read all instructions carefully
  • Start with easier questions to build confidence
  • Use relaxation techniques if you feel overwhelmed
üç∫ Substance Use & Peer Pressure

Making informed decisions about alcohol and drugs, resisting peer pressure.

Saying No Without Losing Friends:
  • "I'm the designated driver tonight"
  • "I'm on medication that doesn't mix well"
  • "I want to remember tonight"
  • "I'm good with what I have"
Warning Signs of Problem Use:
  • Using substances to cope with problems
  • Neglecting responsibilities or relationships
  • Risky behavior while under the influence
  • Inability to have fun without substances
Getting Help:

SAMHSA National Helpline: 1-800-662-4357 (free, confidential, 24/7)

ü§ù How to Help a Friend

Sometimes the best way to help yourself is by helping someone else. Here's how to support a friend who might be struggling.

üö® Signs Your Friend Might Need Help

  • Withdrawing from friends and activities they used to enjoy
  • Sudden changes in mood, sleep, or eating habits
  • Talking about feeling hopeless or worthless
  • Increased irritability or anger
  • Declining academic performance
  • Self-harm or risky behaviors

üí¨ What to Say (and What NOT to Say)

‚úÖ Do Say:
  • "I've noticed you've seemed down lately. Want to talk about it?"
  • "I'm here for you, no matter what."
  • "You don't have to go through this alone."
  • "Your feelings are important to me."
‚ùå Don't Say:
  • "Just get over it."
  • "Everyone feels like this sometimes."
  • "You're being dramatic."
  • "I don't know how to help you."

üìû When to Get Adult Help

Get immediate help if your friend:

  • Talks about harming themselves or others
  • Shows signs of serious depression or anxiety
  • Is using drugs or alcohol to cope
  • Is being abused or bullied severely

Remember: It's better to overreact than to underreact. You could save a life.

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Is it really anonymous?

Yes! We take your privacy seriously. Unless there's an immediate safety concern, everything you share stays between you and our counselors. No names, no tracking, no judgment.

What happens if I call or chat?

You'll be connected with a trained counselor who will listen without judgment. They'll ask gentle questions to understand your situation and offer support and resources. You control the conversation.

What if I don't know what to say?

That's completely normal! You can start with something simple like "I'm feeling overwhelmed" or "I just need someone to listen." Our counselors are trained to help guide the conversation.

Is there a cost?

No, all our services are completely free. We're here to help, not to make money.

Can I text instead of calling?

Absolutely! Many teens prefer texting. You can use our live chat feature or text "HOME" to 741741 for the Crisis Text Line.

What ages do you help?

We primarily support teens ages 13-19, but if you're outside this range and need help, we'll connect you with appropriate resources.
